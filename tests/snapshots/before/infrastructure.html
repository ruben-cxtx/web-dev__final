Infrastructure and Providers

Understand how Panama built its digital backbone: submarine cables, broadband growth, mobile internet, and the evolution of ISPs and mobile operators through competition and mergers.

Infrastructure

Timeline of Infrastructure

early-2000s

ADSL & Cable broadband arrive

Urban Panama gets online primarily via DSL from Cable & Wireless and cable internet from Cable Onda.

2008

New mobile competitors

Claro and Digicel enter, intensifying competition alongside Movistar (+Movil/Cable & Wireless).

2010

‘Internet Para Todos’ / Red Nacional de Internet (RNI)

Government-backed Wi-Fi program begins expanding public access points; complemented by Infoplazas community centers nationwide.

2014–2015

700 MHz awards & first LTE launches

Regulator assigns 700 MHz APT spectrum; +Movil (CWP) and Claro launch 4G LTE, improving coverage and speeds.

2018–2019

Market consolidation (Millicom)

Millicom acquires Cable Onda (2018) and Telefónica/Movistar Panama (2019), later rebranding to Tigo (2020).

2022

Claro integrated into +Movil (CWP)

Cable & Wireless Panamá completes acquisition of Claro Panamá; Digicel files for liquidation and exits the market.

2023

Starlink launches in Panama

LEO satellite broadband becomes available nationwide, complementing rural connectivity initiatives.

2023–2024

5G preparations & pilots

+Movil (CWP) prepares and pilots 5G; ASEP later announces +Movil’s commercial 5G launch in Nov 2024. Tigo prepares 5G as well.

Other important Infrastructures

Subsea Cables

PCCS (Pacific Caribbean Cable System)

Landing in Panama: Balboa (Pacific), María Chiquita (Caribbean)

rfs: 2015

Notes

6000 km trunk; connects Florida–Ecuador with branches; strengthens Panama’s dual-ocean hub role.

Curie (Google)

Landing in Panama: Balboa (via Panama Digital Gateway)

rfs: 2019–2020

Notes

US–Chile private cable with Panama branch; boosts capacity and latency via PDG CLS.

Maya-1

Landing in Panama: María Chiquita (Caribbean)

rfs: 2000

Notes

Regional system linking US–Mexico–Central America–Colombia with a Panama landing.

Pan-American Crossing (PAC)

Landing in Panama: Fort Amador (Balboa)

rfs: 2000

Notes

Self-healing ring linking US–Mexico–Central & South America with a Panama Pacific landing.

South American Crossing (SAC)

Landing in Panama: Colón (Caribbean), Fort Amador (Pacific)

rfs: 2000

Notes

Dual-ocean presence around Panama; now part of larger regional networks.

Spectrum and 5G

Regulator

ASEP

Highlights

  • 700 MHz (APT) awards enable wide-area LTE starting 2014–2015.
  • ASEP announced +Movil’s commercial 5G launch in Nov 2024; Tigo preparing 5G.
  • Operators also utilize PCS (1900 MHz) and AWS (1700/2100 MHz) holdings for 3G/4G.
