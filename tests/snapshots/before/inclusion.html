Digital Inclusion & Government Initiatives

How Panama’s public sector and institutions worked to make the internet accessible and integrate digital services into governance, shifting access from urban elites to broad, inclusive usage across classes and geographies.

Programs

Infoplazas (Community Internet Centers)

What is it?

Physical community centers that provide public internet access, digital skills training, and e-services support across all provinces and several comarcas.

Timeline

2000

Program established under SENACYT (early deployments throughout the 2000s).

2011

Government announces 220 Infoplazas nationwide.

2014

Official list shows at least 222 active Infoplazas (mid-year snapshot).

2021

Infoplazas AIP continues expansion and partnerships (e.g., universities, municipalities).

2025

Infoplazas AIP states '400+ Infoplazas' nationwide.

Metrics

Sites in 2011: 220
Sites in 2014: 222
Sites in 2025: 400
Benefits
  • Expanded access for rural and urban communities
  • Free or very low-cost connectivity and training
  • On-ramps for e-government transactions

Red Nacional de Internet (Internet Para Todos)

What is it?

Nationwide free public Wi-Fi program operated by AIG. RNI 2.0 increases coverage and capacity to enable access to state digital services.

Timeline

2010

Launch of Internet Para Todos (RNI) as a free Wi-Fi network.

2011

Infoplazas and RNI integrated into broader digital inclusion push.

2020

Program modernization and continued expansion via RNI 2.0.

Metrics

Corregimientos Target: 288
Population Reach Percentage: 80
Benefits
  • Nationwide Wi-Fi coverage targeting 80% population reach
  • Focus on underserved corregimientos
  • Supports access to digital government services

Government Initiatives

Institutional milestones

  • 2004

    Creation of the Secretaría de la Presidencia para la Innovación Gubernamental (SIG) by Executive Decree 102.

  • 2009

    Establishment of the Autoridad Nacional para la Innovación Gubernamental (AIG) by Law 65.

  • 2010

    AIG regulation issued by Executive Decree 205.

  • 2012

    E-Government Law (Law 83) mandates digital agendas and regulates online procedures.

Flagship Platforms

  • Panamá en Línea (Trámites en línea)

    Single window for online procedures and payments; supports interoperability and digital signatures.

  • Centro de Atención Ciudadana 311

    Omnichannel citizen service desk assisting with government services and online procedures.

  • Datos Abiertos

    Open data portal for government datasets (e.g., RNI usage).

  • Municipios Digitales

    Local government digital services and modernization initiatives coordinated by AIG.

Strategic Agendas and Plans

  • Name: Plan Estratégico de Banda Ancha (PEBA)

    Years: 2013–2015 (updated)

    Highlights:

    • Guides broadband expansion, inclusion, and service quality
    • Supports open government and citizen-centric services

  • Name: Agenda Digital Estratégica (Panamá 4.0)

    Years: 2014–2019

    Highlights:

    • Interoperability, simplification of procedures, and shared platforms
    • Grounded in Law 83 (2012) and aligned with national plans

  • Name: Agenda Digital / Hoja de Ruta AIG

    Years: 2020–2025

    Highlights:

    • Successive agendas (2020, 2021, 2022) and a 2025 Roadmap continue expansion of coverage, cybersecurity, data governance and interoperability

  • Name: Sectoral Digital Strategies toward 2030

    Years: 2024–2030

    Highlights:

    • Digital Health Strategy 2024–2030 under MINSA with PAHO support
    • National STI policy references Industry 4.0, including AI and IoT enablement
