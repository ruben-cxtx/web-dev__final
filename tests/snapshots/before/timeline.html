Timeline

An overview of Panama’s internet journey from dial-up to 5G. Explore major milestones, broadband expansion, and government policy shaping connectivity from 2001 to 2025.

Key Insights

Penetration

7% → 82%

Panama's internet penetration rose from 7% in 2001 to nearly 82% in 2025, driven by policy reforms, market competition, and public Wi-Fi initiatives.

Public Wi-Fi

Internet para Todos

Government-led programs like 'Internet Para Todos' and Infoplazas provided free access in cities and rural areas, reducing the digital divide.

Mobile

158% (2014)

Panama moved from EDGE in the 2000s to nationwide 4G and early 5G trials by 2024, with 95% population coverage and 118% mobile penetration.

ISP Market

Duopoly by 2022

The telecom sector consolidated into a duopoly (Tigo and C&W+Claro) by 2022 after Digicel's exit. Foreign investment drove infrastructure upgrades.

Digital Strategy

2024–2030 Plan

The 2024–2030 strategic plan promotes AI, IoT, and fintech, aiming for full broadband coverage and a cash-light, digitally inclusive economy.

Timeline

2001

Call Center Law & Early Access

Panama passes the Call Center Incentives Law to boost telecom. INTEL privatized, Cable & Wireless dominates. Internet cafés emerge, ~7% population has internet access.

2004

E-Gov Foundations

The government launches the Secretariat for Government Innovation (later AIG). Dial-up begins to fade as ADSL and cable broadband grow in urban areas.

2006–2008

Mobile Competition Expands

Internet use grows to 34%. Digicel and Claro enter in 2008, intensifying mobile competition. Broadband subscriptions increase across the country.

2010

Internet Para Todos

Government launches free public Wi-Fi. By 2014, 1,100+ hotspots are live in 41 cities. Internet access exceeds 40% of the population.

2010s Early

Infoplazas Expand

Infoplazas grow from 214 (2011) to 312 centers by mid-decade. These centers bridge the digital divide in libraries, schools, and communities.

2013–2015

4G Rollout Begins

Panama launches 4G LTE (2014–15), mobile penetration hits 158%. Broadband subscriptions top 300,000. Cable modem becomes dominant fixed access.

2018

Millicom Enters Broadband

Millicom (Tigo) acquires 80% of Cable Onda, the largest cable/fixed operator. Internet penetration reaches 62% with significant foreign investment.

2019

Tigo Acquires Movistar

Millicom (Tigo) expands into mobile, acquiring Movistar Panamá. Internet users rise to 64% of population, strengthening convergence in services.

2020

Pandemic Drives Demand

COVID-19 pushes internet penetration beyond 70%. Remote work and school drive demand. ISPs expand rural coverage and improve bandwidth.

2021–2022

ISP Market Consolidation

Claro merges with Cable & Wireless in 2022. Digicel exits Panama. Tigo and C&W+Claro dominate the market. Internet access hits ~74%.

2023

Mobile & Fiber Growth

4G covers 95% of the population. Fiber rolls out in cities. Over 5.3M mobile lines (118% penetration). Internet users reach ~3.5M (79%).

2024

5G Tests & Digital Plans

Operators begin 5G trials. Panama unveils 2024–2030 Digital Strategy (AI, IoT, fintech). RNI 2.0 and Infoplazas continue expanding access.

2025

Digital Equity Push

Panama launches rural fiber expansion with satellite support. Digital literacy programs increase. Internet penetration nears 82%, aiming for universal coverage.
